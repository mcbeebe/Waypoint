Competitor Deep Dives

Undivided (Primary Competitor)

  • Model: Human parent-navigators trained in CA disability systems
  • Funding: $9.4M raised (Barrel, Red Sea, StartUp Health)
  • Pricing: Platform $9/mo (content library), Platform Plus $49/mo (messaging), Navigator $115/mo (1:1 dedicated)
  • Key Insight: The $9/mo comparison is misleading. Undivided's $9 tier is a content library. Waypoint's $9.99 Plus is a full AI agent that reads IEPs, drafts appeals, and identifies missed benefits. The correct comparison is Waypoint Plus ($9.99) vs. Undivided Navigator ($115/mo).
  • Vulnerability: Scale economics. Each Undivided Navigator serves 30-50 families. Reaching 10,000 families requires 200-300 Navigators. Waypoint serves the 10,000th family at the same marginal cost as the 10th.

Eleplan (Emerging Competitor)

  • Model: AI-powered care planning platform for families and providers
  • Funding: Closing $1M pre-seed (as of early 2026)
  • Pricing: Free tier + $15/mo paid
  • Differentiator: HIPAA-compliant vault, medication tracking, CES 2025 Innovation recognition
  • Gap vs. Waypoint: Organizes care information but does not navigate benefits systems (RC, IEP, SSI, IHSS). No California-specific legal knowledge. No multi-language support.

Other Players

  • Findhelp (Aunt Bertha): National social services directory ($54M raised). Generic, no AI, no disability specialization. Passive resource matching.
  • Joshin (now RethinkFirst): Acquired Aug 2025. Employer-focused disability support. B2B model, not family navigation.
  • Exceptional Lives: Nonprofit with 2,500+ resources and 70M+ reach. Content library, not AI navigation. No California-specific systems guidance.
  • Playground IEP: Free AI IEP writing tool for teachers. School-focused, not parent-facing. Limited to IEP workflows.
  • SpedTrack: School district IEP management software. Enterprise B2B, not consumer. No broader disability navigation.
  • Therap Services: Provider documentation platform (80+ modules). Agency software, not family-facing.

Building a Defensible Moat

A sustainable competitive advantage requires multiple reinforcing layers. Here are the seven moat strategies, ranked by defensibility and feasibility:

1. Proprietary Outcome Data (Strongest Moat)

  • What: Every family interaction generates structured data on which strategies work for which Regional Centers, insurers, diagnoses, and demographics
  • Why it matters: Over time, Waypoint learns that RCEB approves ABA faster with IPP amendment language X, while NLACRC requires documentation Y. This knowledge cannot be replicated without serving thousands of families.
  • Defensibility: Extremely high. This data does not exist anywhere. Facebook groups have anecdotal fragments; Waypoint has structured, anonymized, outcome-tracked data across all 21 RCs.
  • Action: Instrument every action completion, draft usage, and outcome (service approved/denied) from Day 1. Build an anonymous insights engine that feeds back into AI recommendations.

2. Knowledge Base Depth + Legal Precision

  • What: 49 KB articles today; target 150+ covering every RC, diagnosis, and multi-system scenario with exact statute citations (W&I 4646, IDEA 300.303, etc.)
  • Why it matters: Legal precision is the trust foundation. Parents share Waypoint because it cited the exact law that got their child services. No competitor has RAG-structured, citation-verified disability law content.
  • Action: Engage disability rights attorneys for quarterly content review. Build a content pipeline with contract KB writers post-MVP. Version-control every article.

3. RC Reimbursement Channel Lock-In

  • What: Get Waypoint listed as an approved vendor under POS codes 371/331 (Parent Education/Support Services) at each Regional Center
  • Why it matters: Once a family's IPP or SDP spending plan includes Waypoint, switching costs are high. RC coordinators recommend Waypoint by name. This is the zero-CAC acquisition flywheel.
  • Action: Start with RCEB (home market). Document outcomes rigorously. Use early success stories to expand to all 21 RCs. Target Service Coordinator champions.

4. Community Network Effects

  • What: Diagnosis-specific forums, local parent groups, provider-family coordination within Waypoint
  • Why it matters: Special needs communities are the most tight-knit and share-aggressive communities on the internet. If parents find their tribe inside Waypoint, switching to a competitor means leaving their support network.
  • Action: Seed community with power users from Facebook groups. Create diagnosis-specific channels. Add moderation (critical for trust). Launch after core AI navigation is proven.

5. Multi-System Integration Complexity

  • What: Waypoint understands how RC, IEP, insurance, Medi-Cal, IHSS, SSI, and SDP all interact and who pays first
  • Why it matters: The payer-of-last-resort logic alone saves families thousands. No competitor maps these cross-system interactions. Building this knowledge takes years of research and real-world validation.
  • Action: Continue deepening the funding waterfall logic. Add outcome tracking for multi-system scenarios. Document the decision trees that generate the most value for families.

6. Provider Marketplace (B2B Lock-In)

  • What: Therapists, attorneys, and financial planners listed in Waypoint's directory, referring clients, and paying for listings
  • Why it matters: Two-sided marketplace effects: more providers attract more families, and more families attract more providers. Once providers build referral workflows around Waypoint, switching costs are high.
  • Action: Start with 5 OT/ABA practices. Offer free access for their clients. Build to 25 paying partners by Month 6. Create 'Certified Waypoint Provider' badge program.

7. Tone Calibration IP

  • What: The collaborative-to-assertive-to-adversarial tone system with diagnosis-aware, agency-specific prompt engineering
  • Why it matters: Parents need different communication styles for different situations. A friendly IEP request vs. a formal 4731 complaint require fundamentally different approaches. This prompt engineering IP is hard to replicate without extensive testing.
  • Action: Continue refining tone system based on user feedback and outcomes. A/B test tone effectiveness. This is a defensible asset that improves with every interaction.

Maximizing Parent Impact

The core question: how can Waypoint help parents the most? Based on the market research, user personas, and caregiver burden data, here are the highest-impact strategies ranked by urgency and feasibility.

1. Solve the "I Don't Know What I Don't Know" Problem

The single biggest value Waypoint delivers is revealing benefits families didn't know existed. Research shows families leave tens of thousands of dollars in unclaimed services on the table.

  • Eligibility engine: 60-second onboarding reveals 5-15 programs the family qualifies for but hasn't applied to
  • Funding waterfall: Maps which entity pays first (insurance, Medi-Cal, CCS, then RC) so families don't pay out-of-pocket for covered services
  • Proactive nudges: "Your child turns 3 in 4 months. Here's what changes and what to file now."

2. Remove Parent Paralysis with Scripts and Drafts

Parents know they need to advocate but freeze when facing bureaucracy. Waypoint's greatest feature is not answers but the exact words to say.

  • Every action includes a script: "Call your Service Coordinator and say: 'I am requesting an IPP amendment under W&I Section 4646 to add ABA therapy.'"
  • Draft generation: 12 templates that produce ready-to-send emails and letters with the family's specific details pre-filled
  • Tone escalation: Start collaborative. If denied, Waypoint generates an assertive follow-up. If still denied, an adversarial complaint citing specific legal violations.

3. Reduce Caregiver Burnout

33% of informal caregivers experience clinical depression. 1 in 5 are at risk of burnout. Waypoint can directly reduce the administrative burden that drives this.

  • Deadline tracking: Auto-calculates every legal deadline (IEP review, insurance appeal window, SSI redetermination) so parents stop worrying about what they're missing
  • Document vault: One place for IEPs, evaluations, insurance denials. No more frantic searching through email and filing cabinets before meetings
  • Emotional check-ins: Track frustration levels, offer encouragement, connect to peer support when needed

4. Serve Underserved Families

The equity analysis reveals significant racial/ethnic disparities in RC service distribution. Non-English-speaking and low-income families face the steepest barriers.

  • Full Spanish + Vietnamese support: Both UI and AI responses, not just translated labels but culturally appropriate guidance
  • RC POS data transparency: Show families the Purchase of Service data proving disparities, empowering them to advocate for equitable services
  • RC reimbursement: Makes Waypoint free for families whose RC covers the cost, removing the financial barrier entirely

5. Bridge the Gap at Key Transitions

Families face the highest stress at age transitions: birth-to-3, 3-to-school, elementary-to-middle, and the critical 18-22 adult transition.

  • Age-triggered guidance: Waypoint knows the child's birthday and proactively surfaces what changes and what to do before each transition
  • Transition planning: DOR vocational services, ABLE accounts, conservatorship alternatives, SDP enrollment
  • Multi-year journey maps: Visual roadmaps showing where the family is and what's coming next, reducing the overwhelming feeling of an infinite to-do list

Strategic Recommendations

Priority 1: Ship the Mobile App (Weeks 1-8)

The GAS MVP proved the concept. Now the 2.0 React Native app must ship to reach families where they are: on their phones, at 11pm, the night before an IEP meeting.

  • Port the AI Navigator, action plan engine, and draft generator first (core value)
  • Add Apple Sign-In + Supabase Auth (table stakes for App Store)
  • Migrate KB articles and prompts to Supabase + pgvector (RAG architecture)
  • Launch on TestFlight with 100 beta families from Facebook groups

Priority 2: Prove RC Reimbursement (Weeks 5-16)

This is the single most important business validation. If RCs will pay for Waypoint, the business model is proven and CAC drops to near-zero.

  • Document outcomes from beta testers (hours saved, services obtained, appeals won)
  • Create one-pager for Service Coordinators explaining Waypoint's value
  • Secure pilot agreement with RCEB for 50-100 families
  • Build RC admin dashboard showing family usage and outcomes

Priority 3: Build the Data Moat (Ongoing)

Start collecting structured outcome data from Day 1. This is the long-term competitive advantage that compounds over time.

  • Instrument every action completion, draft usage, and service outcome
  • Build anonymous insights engine ("Families at RCEB who used script X got approved 73% of the time")
  • Create quarterly outcome reports for RC partners and investors

Priority 4: Expand KB to 150+ Articles (Weeks 9-26)

  • Cover all 21 Regional Centers with RC-specific guides (forms, processes, culture)
  • Add advanced topics: appeals, multi-system scenarios, special needs trusts, ABLE accounts
  • Engage disability rights attorney for quarterly content review
  • Build content pipeline with contract KB writers

Priority 5: Launch Community (Weeks 17-26)

Community is the retention and engagement multiplier. But only launch after core AI navigation is proven.

  • Seed with power users from Facebook groups (they're already looking for a better platform)
  • Create diagnosis-specific channels (autism, ADHD, Down syndrome, etc.)
  • Add moderation tooling (critical for trust in a vulnerable community)
  • Enable provider participation (verified badge, Q&A, not advertising)
